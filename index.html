Research Publication

House Price Prediction Using Regression Model (UGC Journal – Fuzzy Systems and Soft Computing, Vol. 19, Issue 02)

I authored and presented a research paper titled "House Price Prediction Using Regression Model", focused on machine learning applications in real estate. The paper was published in a UGC-recognized journal and also presented at an internal academic conference attended by faculty and peers. My research explored the use of Linear Regression, Ridge, Lasso, and Random Forest to predict housing prices from historical data. Key features analyzed included location, square footage, room count, and amenities. I applied data cleaning, normalization, and feature selection to enhance model performance. Evaluation metrics such as R², MAE, and RMSE were used to measure accuracy and reliability. Random Forest Regression emerged as the best-performing model, providing strong predictive results. The experience improved my proficiency in Python (scikit-learn, pandas), statistical analysis, and report writing. Presenting the paper at the conference helped build my confidence in public speaking and technical communication. This project highlighted my interest in data-driven decision-making and applied machine learning research.
